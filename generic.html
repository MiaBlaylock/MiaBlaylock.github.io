Just a bit about me

Senior at UC Berkeley completing a Simultaneous Degree: BA Data Science with emphasis: geospatial information & technology, BA Earth and Planetary Science

I'm currently a senior completing a simultaneous degree at UC Berkeley's College of Computing and Data Science and Society with a B.A. in Data Science with an emphasis in geospatial information and technology and a B.A. in Environmental Earth Science from UC Berkeley's College of Letters and Sciences. My academic journey, which includes coursework in Artificial Intelligence, Deep Learning, Data Structures & Algorithms, and Principles & Techniques of Data Science, has equipped me with a strong foundation in computational methods and software development. This technical background, combined with specialized courses in Earth Sience such as Earth System Remote Sensing, Quantitative Environmental Analysis, and Geomorpholohy, reflects my commitment to bridging the gap between advanced computing and environmental science.

Currently, I serve as an Undergraduate Researcher in the Environmental System Design Lab, collaborating with the US Army Corps of Engineers to develop physics-informed machine learning models for hydrological forecasting. This role has allowed me to apply my expertise in Python, machine learning, and data analysis to real-world environmental challenges while gaining valuable experience in high-performance computing and large-scale data processing. While my research demonstrates my passion for environmental applications, my strong technical foundation in AI, deep learning, and data structures positions me to tackle complex computational problems across various domains, from environmental modeling to broader applications in data science and machine learning.
